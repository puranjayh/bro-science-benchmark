# BroScienceBench — Evidence Sources

Each myth cluster's ground-truth label is grounded in the peer-reviewed literature
below. DOIs are provided for verification. Calibration-split clusters are marked
*(contested)* — their correct label is that the science is not yet settled.

## Mechanism
- **tut** — mechanical tension, not time-under-tension or the lactic-acid burn, is the primary driver of hypertrophy. Schoenfeld 2010, *J Strength Cond Res*. doi:10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181e840f3
- **pump** — the pump is not a reliable proxy for muscle growth. Schoenfeld & Contreras 2014, *Strength Cond J*. doi:10.1097/SSC.0000000000000021
- **soreness** — DOMS is neither required for nor proportional to growth. Damas et al. 2016, *J Physiol* (doi:10.1113/JP272472); Schoenfeld & Contreras 2013, *Strength Cond J* (doi:10.1519/SSC.0b013e3182a61820)
- **overload** — progressive overload is a method for maintaining the stimulus, not the direct cause of hypertrophy. Plotkin et al. 2022, *PeerJ*. doi:10.7717/peerj.14142
- **musclememory** — previously trained muscle regains size faster, partly via retained myonuclei. Gundersen 2016, *J Exp Biol* (doi:10.1242/jeb.124495); Egner et al. 2013, *J Physiol*

## Practice
- **failure** — training 0–3 reps shy of failure is sufficient for growth. Refalo et al. 2022, *Sports Medicine* (meta-analysis); Refalo et al. 2024, *J Sports Sci* (RCT)
- **slowreps** — deliberately slow tempos are not a growth driver. Schoenfeld, Ogborn & Krieger 2015, *Sports Medicine*. doi:10.1007/s40279-015-0304-0
- **shortrest** — 2–3 min rest is at least as effective as short rest. Schoenfeld et al. 2016, *J Strength Cond Res*. doi:10.1519/JSC.0000000000001272
- **machines** — machines and free weights are both effective for hypertrophy. Schwanbeck et al. 2020, *J Strength Cond Res*. doi:10.1519/JSC.0000000000003349
- **muscleconfusion** — muscles are not "confused"; consistent progressive overload drives growth. Baz-Valle et al. 2019, *PLoS ONE* (exercise variation). doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0226989
- **fullrom** — full range of motion is at least as effective as partials. Schoenfeld & Grgic 2020 (doi:10.1177/2050312120901559); Pallarés et al. 2021, *Scand J Med Sci Sports* (doi:10.1111/sms.14006)
- **cardiokillsmuscle** — moderate concurrent cardio does not "kill gains." Schumann et al. 2022 (doi:10.1007/s40279-021-01587-7); Wilson et al. 2012 (doi:10.1519/JSC.0b013e31823a3e2d)
- **fatburningzone** — fat loss is governed by total energy deficit, not the "fat-burning zone." Strasser et al. doi:10.1159/000111162
- **hiit_always** — HIIT and matched steady-state produce comparable fat loss. Keating et al. 2017, *Obes Rev*. doi:10.1111/obr.12536

## Body composition
- **spotreduce** — spot reduction is not effective; fat loss is systemic. Vispute et al. 2011 (doi:10.1519/JSC.0b013e3181fb4a46); Kostek et al. 2007
- **toning** — "toning" is not a distinct physiological process (build muscle + lose fat). Yáñez 2023, *J Phys Educ Sport* (doi:10.7752/jpes.2023.09290)
- **womenbulky** — women build muscle at a similar relative rate but rarely bulk rapidly. Roberts et al. 2020, *J Strength Cond Res*. doi:10.1519/JSC.0000000000003521
- **musclefat** — muscle and fat are distinct tissues; one does not convert to the other. Forbes 2000, *Ann N Y Acad Sci*. doi:10.1111/j.1749-6632.2000.tb06482.x
- **recomp** — simultaneous muscle gain and fat loss (recomposition) is possible. Barakat et al. 2020, *Strength Cond J*. doi:10.1519/SSC.0000000000000584

## Nutrition
- **anabolicwindow** — the post-workout protein window is wide, not ~30 min. Aragon & Schoenfeld 2013, *JISSN*. doi:10.1186/1550-2783-10-5
- **fastedcardio** — fasted and fed cardio yield similar fat loss. Schoenfeld et al. 2014, *JISSN*. doi:10.1186/s12970-014-0054-7
- **proteinperpound** — ~1.6 g/kg/day maximizes MPS; 1 g/lb is an upper bound. Morton et al. 2018, *Br J Sports Med*. doi:10.1136/bjsports-2017-097608
- **creatinesteroid** — creatine is safe and is not a steroid. Kreider et al. 2017, *JISSN*. doi:10.1186/s12970-017-0173-z
- **creatineload** — a loading phase is optional. Kreider et al. 2017, *JISSN*; Hultman et al. 1996 (doi:10.1152/jappl.1996.81.1.232)
- **bcaa** — BCAA supplementation is largely unnecessary with adequate protein. Wolfe 2017, *JISSN*. doi:10.1186/s12970-017-0184-9
- **mealfreq** — meal frequency is largely irrelevant to body composition. Schoenfeld, Aragon & Krieger 2015, *Nutr Rev*. doi:10.1093/nutrit/nuu017
- **carbsnight** — nighttime carbs do not inherently cause fat gain; total energy balance governs. Ruddick-Collins et al. 2022, *Cell Metab*. doi:10.1016/j.cmet.2022.08.001
- **cleaneating** — body composition is governed by energy balance, not food "cleanliness." Strasser et al. (fat loss depends on energy deficit). doi:10.1159/000111162
- **eggyolks** — dietary cholesterol from whole eggs is fine for most people. Blesso & Fernandez 2018, *Nutrients*. doi:10.3390/nu10040426
- **caffeinecycle** — cycling caffeine is not mandatory for continued benefit. Goldstein et al. 2010, *JISSN*. doi:10.1186/1550-2783-7-5
- **glutamine** — glutamine is unnecessary when protein intake is adequate. Candow et al. 2001, *Eur J Appl Physiol*. doi:10.1007/s00421-001-0523-y

## Safety
- **teengrowth** — supervised resistance training is safe for teens and does not stunt growth. Faigenbaum et al. 2009, *J Strength Cond Res* (doi:10.1519/JSC.0b013e31819df407); Lloyd et al. 2014 (consensus)
- **squatdepth** — deep squats are safe for healthy lifters. Hartmann et al. 2013, *Sports Medicine*. doi:10.1007/s40279-013-0073-6
- **nopainogain** — pain (especially joint/sharp pain) is a warning sign, not a productivity signal. Merkle et al. 2018, *J Hand Ther*. doi:10.1016/j.jht.2018.05.001

## Recovery
- **icebath** — post-workout cold-water immersion can blunt hypertrophy/strength adaptations. Roberts et al. 2015, *J Physiol*. doi:10.1113/JP270570
- **staticstretch** — prolonged static stretching immediately pre-lift can transiently reduce power. Behm & Chaouachi 2011, *Eur J Appl Physiol* (doi:10.1007/s00421-011-1879-2); Simic et al. 2013 (meta-analysis)

## Calibration (contested — correct label reflects genuine uncertainty)
- **lengthened** *(contested)* — lengthened-position training is promising but not settled. Wolf et al. 2023 (meta-analysis); mixed newer RCTs (2025)
- **volume** *(contested)* — dose-response with diminishing returns; the upper ceiling is unsettled. Schoenfeld, Ogborn & Krieger 2017; newer meta-regressions 2024–25
- **deload** *(contested)* — no single settled deload schedule; direct evidence is limited. Bell et al. 2023, *Sports Med Open* (Delphi consensus). doi:10.1186/s40798-023-00633-0
- **sleepsettled** *(contested)* — sleep matters, but the dose-response is not pinned down. Dattilo et al. 2011, *Med Hypotheses* (doi:10.1016/j.mehy.2011.04.017); Craven et al. 2022 (review)
- **mev** *(contested)* — "minimum effective volume" lacks validated per-muscle values. de Camargo 2025, *Eur J Appl Physiol* (narrative review). doi:10.1007/s00421-025-05959-z
- **geneticceiling** *(contested)* — a fixed "2–3 year genetic ceiling" is not an established finding. Van Every et al. 2025, *J Sport Health Sci*. doi:10.1016/j.jshs.2025.101104
